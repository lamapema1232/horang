Application Process

Eligibility criteria for Admission in 10+2 programs: Students with a GPA of 2.0 and SEE grades of C+ in math and science and D+ in social studies, Nepali, and English may apply for the Science stream.
Students in the Management, Law, and Humanities streams should have a GPA of 1.6 and a D+ in Math, Science, Social Studies, English, and Nepali in SEE.
Documents required for Admission in 10+2 programs:
  • SEE/SLC Mark sheet, Transcripts.
  • School Leaving Certificate SEE/SLC character and migration.
  • Foreign Equivalency Certification (FEC) (in case of any).

Eligibility criteria for Admission to undergraduate programs:
A CGPA of at least 1.8 and a minimum D+ mark in each subject in grades 11 and 12.
Or,
Students can also apply if they are awaiting their grade 11 or grade 12 results. They must, however, turn in the findings at the time of Admission.
Documents required for Admission to the undergraduate programs:
  • Individual Mark sheet of 11th and 12th-grade and 10+2 Transcript.
  • Character, Provisional, and Migration Certificate of 10+2.
  • SEE/SLC Mark sheet, Transcripts.
  • Certificate of entrance examination passed.

Eligibility criteria for Admission to graduate programs:
Candidates for the Xavier International College graduates' program must have completed at least 15 years of formal education (12 years of schooling plus three years of graduation).
The applicant must also have a minimum CGPA of 2.0 or 45 percent at the bachelor's level.
Documents required for Admission to the graduate programs:
  • All academic transcripts with attestations and character certificates (copy) (SLC onwards).
  • Original Bachelor's Degree Migration Certificate (If any academic degree from a foreign university, a copy of the equivalent certificate).
  • Copy of citizenship certificate with authentication.
  • Two copies of recent passport-size photos.

College Uniform

College uniforms:are a types of dress code that requires astudents to wear a standardized outfit or attire while attending college. The uniform typically consists specific clothing items,colors,and accessories that students are required to wear. The purpose of college uniforms may vary; but some reasons why colleges implement them are:

Promoting a sense of unity and identity:college uniforms promote a sense of unity and identity among students. By wearing the same clothes, students can feel like they are part of acommunity and have ashared sense of purposes

Reducing distraction and competition:Uniforms can help reduce distraction and competition among students. Without the pressure to keep up with the latest fashion trends or expensive clothing, students can focus on their studies and feel more comfortablr in their own skin.

Fostering discipline and respect:Wearing a uniform can foster discipline nad respect among students. By adhering to adress, code, studens can learn to follow rules and guidelines, which can be valuable in their future professional lives.

Creating aprofessional appearance:College uniforms can create aprofessional appearance and help prepare students for the workforce. By dressing in auniforms, students can learn to present themslves in a polished and a professional manner.
